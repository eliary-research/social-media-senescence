### 3.2 Why Google Was Less Affected

1. **First-party search intent data**: Google's core advertising product is Search, which relies on user queries (first-party data) rather than cross-app tracking. Users explicitly tell Google what they want. ATT restricts cross-app/cross-site tracking, which barely affects Search.

2. **Owned-and-operated ecosystem**: Google controls the browser (Chrome), the search engine, the mobile OS (Android), YouTube, Gmail, Maps. This gives it massive first-party data that doesn't depend on third-party tracking.

3. **Android not affected**: ATT is iOS-only. Google's Android retained its advertising identifier (GAID) without an opt-in requirement during this period. Google later announced Privacy Sandbox for Android as a "less blunt" alternative.

4. **YouTube impact was real but smaller**: YouTube did see some impact on ad measurement and targeting for iOS users, but this was a smaller portion of Alphabet's total revenue.

**Sources**: CPO Magazine, The Register, Daring Fireball, multiple analyst reports

## 5. Lotame Study: Combined Platform Revenue Loss

**Source**: Lotame (advertising technology firm), reported by Financial Times (Oct 2021)

**Finding**: Facebook, YouTube, Twitter, and Snap lost an estimated **$9.85 billion** in combined advertising revenue in H2 2021 (Q3+Q4) due to ATT.

**Breakdown** [REPORTED — Gizmodo, AppleInsider, BGR]:
- **Facebook**: Largest absolute loss (~$8B+ of the total), due to market size
- **Snap**: Worst hit as a percentage of revenue, due to smartphone-only business
- Revenue across the four companies was down approximately **12%** in aggregate during this period

**Meanwhile**: Apple's own advertising business grew, with Services revenue reaching $18.3B in Q4 2021.

**Sources**:
- AppleInsider: https://appleinsider.com/articles/21/10/31/social-media-firms-see-10b-cut-in-ad-revenue-due-to-app-tracking-transparency
- Gizmodo: https://gizmodo.com/apples-privacy-policy-cost-snap-facebook-twitter-and-1847971994

## 7. ATT Opt-In Rates

### 7.1 Flurry Analytics Data (most widely cited)

| Period | US Opt-In Rate | Worldwide Opt-In Rate | Source |
|--------|---------------|----------------------|--------|
| May 2021 (launch) | **4%** | **11%** | Flurry Analytics |
| Aug 2021 | — | ~25% | Flurry Analytics |
| End of 2021 | — | ~25% | Flurry Analytics |
| March-April 2022 | — | ~25% | Flurry Analytics (last updated May 2, 2022) |

**Important methodological note**: These rates measure ALL iOS 14.5+ users, including those whose apps never showed the ATT prompt (counted as "not opted in"). This is why rates appear very low.

### 7.2 Among Users Who Actually Saw the Prompt

| Source | Opt-In Rate (prompted users only) |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| Kochava | **48.8%** (May 7, 2021 data) |
| Flurry | ~25% of prompted users worldwide |

**Key insight for academic use**: The "4% US opt-in" headline number (Flurry) vs "48.8% among prompted users" (Kochava) shows dramatic methodological sensitivity. The true behavioral opt-in rate among users who actually saw the prompt is much higher than headline numbers suggest.

### 7.3 Tracking Rate Impact (Academic Source)

From Kraft, Bleier, Skiera, and Koschella (2023, SSRN):
- In the US, tracking rates decreased by **54.73 percentage points** (from 72.63% to 17.90%)
- Trackable ad impressions commanded **51.10% higher prices** than non-trackable
- Net effect: advertising revenues decreased by **20.44%** for Apple/iOS users

**Source**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4598472

## 8. Academic Papers on ATT Impact

### 8.1 Published in Peer-Reviewed Journals

**Aridor, G., Che, Y.-K., Hollenbeck, B., McCarthy, D., & Kaiser, M. (2024)**
"Evaluating the Impact of Privacy Regulation on E-Commerce Firms: Evidence from Apple's App Tracking Transparency"
*Management Science* (2024). DOI: 10.1287/mnsc.2024.06600

Key findings:
- Conversion-optimized Meta ads saw **37% reduction in click-through rates** post-ATT
- Firms shifted ad spending from Meta to Google ecosystem
- Firms with higher baseline Meta dependence experienced **8-40% revenue decline**
- Smaller e-commerce firms bore the largest impact
- 26.2% reduction in orders and 24.7% reduction in revenue from new customers

**URL**: https://pubsonline.informs.org/doi/10.1287/mnsc.2024.06600

### 8.2 Working Papers / SSRN

**Kraft, L., Bleier, A., Skiera, B., & Koschella, F. (2023)**
"Granular Control and Privacy Decisions: Evidence from Apple's App Tracking Transparency (ATT)"
SSRN Working Paper #4598472

Key findings:
- US tracking rates: 72.63% → 17.90% (54.73pp decrease)
- 51% price premium for trackable impressions
- 20.44% decline in ad revenue for iOS users
- Analysis across 19 countries using 3 proprietary datasets

**URL**: https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4598472

## 10. Data Gaps & Caveats for Academic Use

1. **Meta's $10B figure is an estimate, not audited**: CFO Wehner explicitly said "We can't be precise on this." Do not treat it as an exact accounting figure.

2. **Separating ATT from macro**: Q2-Q4 2022 declines at Meta were driven by multiple factors: ATT, macroeconomic slowdown, TikTok competition, strong dollar, Reels transition. Isolating ATT's causal effect requires econometric methods (see Aridor et al. 2024).

3. **Opt-in rates are methodologically sensitive**: The "4% US opt-in" vs "49% among prompted users" discrepancy matters. Always specify methodology when citing.

4. **Complete quarterly DAU for Meta Q1-Q3 2020 and Q1-Q3 2021**: Not extractable from web searches. Must be pulled from SEC 10-Q filings directly.

5. **Pinterest ATT-specific data is thin**: Consider dropping Pinterest from the analysis or using it only as a brief mention.

6. **Google's advertising revenue breakdown by quarter** (Search vs YouTube vs Network) was not extracted for 2020. Available in Alphabet 10-K but requires direct SEC access.
